Introduction To The Homeless Mapping Project

Homelessness is an increasingly important topic in relation to civil equality amongts cititzens of the U.S. The U.S. government has spend several billion dollars each year on resrouces for homeless people. Estimates range between $2.9 billion in 2009 to $4.7 billion in 2013. In certain areas the efforts to prevent homelessness from occurring are working. In other areas, however, these efforts are coming back futile and homelessness continues to rise.


The most in depth report of homelessness in the U.S. was conducted by the National Alliance to End Homelessness who conducted a comprehensive study of homelessness across the county in 2015. The 82 page executive report can be found here.


This site will help monitor the location of homeless people over time throughout the county by allowing users to input various attributes. Understanding the spatial distribution of homeless populations over time will allow resources to be allocated efficiently.
